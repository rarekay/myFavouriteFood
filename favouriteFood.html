My Favourite Food

When it comes to favourites, I tend not to have one so I don't make the other options feel left out. For this lesson though, I'll be taking us through how I make my Yam and Egg

The Origin

Yam is the common name for some plant species in the genus Dioscorea (family Dioscoreaceae) that form edible tubers. Yams are perennial herbaceous vines cultivated for the consumption of their starchy tubers in many temperate and tropical regions, especially in West Africa, South America and the Caribbean, Asia, and Oceania. Eggs are somewhat general and the origins of frying comes from Ancient Egypt and French cultures. Yam and egg together is one of the breakfast staples in Nigeria or should I say weekend breakfast.

Ingredients Needed

Items Quantity/Measurements
Yam Tubers 1
Eggs 6
Medium Sized Tomatoes 2
2 Scotch bonnets (atarodo) 1
small onion 1
Red bell pepper (tatashe) 1
Vegetable oil Half cup
Salt One hand half tsp
Seasoning Cube 1
Curry Half tsp
Thyme Half tsp

How to Prepare

Yam

Slice yam into about 1-inch size and peel the skin off. Rinse in clean water till water runs clear and no residue in water.Put yam in a pan, add enough water to cover it, add salt and place on medium heat and cook till soft. Test with a fork, if it goes through, then the yam should be soft enough. Drain water from yam if you want.

Egg

Break eggs into a shallow bowl, whisk till egg white and egg yolk are well combined, add salt and whisk to combine then set it aside
Place a frying pan on a medium/low heat, add the vegetable oil, heat for about 2-3 minutes and then add the chopped peppers and onions (this should sizzle) and cook for about 4-5 minutes or till oil floats on the sauce, add salt, seasoning, curry and thyme. Stir to combine ingredient with sauce and also avoid sauce sticking to the pan.
Add whisked eggs to sauce and leave for about a minute, this will allow the egg to set a bit. Stir to scramble egg. Serve egg sauce with boiled yam immediately.
